Privacy Policy

Welcome to the privacy policy of Cafe Velodrome, effective as of 01/02/2025. We value your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, storage, protection, access, modification, and deletion of your personal information. Our aim is to ensure that you are fully informed about how your data is handled during your use of our services.

This comprehensive privacy statement has been drafted in compliance with all applicable regulations in Europe, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other pertinent data protection laws. We have structured this document to provide you with a transparent overview of our data practices, maintaining compliance with legal requirements and industry best practices.

1. Data Collection

At Cafe Velodrome, we collect various types of information in order to enhance your experience with our services. The types of personal data we may gather include:

  • Identifiers (e.g., name, unique user ID, login credentials)
  • Contact data (e.g., email address, mailing address, phone numbers)
  • Online identifiers and tracking data collected via cookies or similar technologies
  • Usage data and preferences, which are voluntarily provided through our service interactions
  • Technical data such as IP addresses, browser type, operating system, and device identifiers

We only process data that is necessary for the effective and lawful delivery of our services. Each data processing activity is designed to respect your privacy rights and is conducted in accordance with the principle of data minimization.

2. Use of Personal Data

Your personal data is processed for multiple reasons, all of which are essential for the performance of our services as well as for improving and personalizing your user experience. We may use your data to:

  1. Provide, maintain, and enhance our services;
  2. Understand and analyze how you use our service so that we can develop new products, services, and features;
  3. Monitor and prevent fraudulent or illegal activities;
  4. Communicate updates, promotional offers, and other relevant information when permitted;
  5. Respond to your inquiries and manage customer support;
  6. Ensure the security and integrity of our systems by conducting audits, data analysis, and identifying breaking patterns;
  7. Comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

By using our service, you explicitly consent to the processing of your personal data in the aforementioned ways. Every processing activity is carried out with a commitment to maintaining transparency and fairness in all our operations.

3. Legal Bases for Data Processing

In accordance with data protection regulations, our processing of your personal data is based on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: Where you have given your explicit consent to specific data processing activities.
  • Contractual Necessity: When the processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which you are a party.
  • Legal Obligation: Where we are legally obliged to process your data.
  • Legitimate Interest: When the processing is necessary for the purposes of legitimate interests pursued by Cafe Velodrome, except where such interests are overridden by your data protection rights.

Although we process your data based on one or more of these legal bases, we are committed to ensuring that all necessary safeguards are in place, and that your data is processed securely and responsibly.

4. Data Storage, Retention, and Security

The security of your personal data is of utmost importance to us. We implement robust technical and organizational measures to protect your data against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected and in compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements. We regularly review our retention policies to ensure that data is not stored longer than necessary.

Security practices include, but are not limited to, encryption, access controls, data anonymization, and continuous system monitoring. These measures are reviewed and updated periodically based on evolving best practices and technological advancements.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We employ cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and to collect data regarding how our site is used. Cookies may be used to:

  • Identify and authenticate users;
  • Remember user preferences and configurations;
  • Analyze website traffic and improve functionality;
  • Deliver personalized content and advertising.

You have the option to manage your cookie preferences at any time. Should you choose to disable cookies, some functionalities of the service may be affected. For detailed information about how we use cookies and how you can manage them, please refer to our cookie policy, which is accessible via a link provided on our website interface.

6. Your Rights Regarding Personal Data

We acknowledge your rights as a data subject and strive to ensure that these rights are respected at all times. You are entitled to the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of Access: You can request access to the personal data we hold about you and be provided with a copy of such data.
  • Right to Rectification: If you believe that any personal data we have about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct or update that information.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Under certain circumstances, you can request the deletion of your personal data from our records.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You may request that we temporarily suspend the processing of your personal data in certain situations.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to have that data transferred to another controller when technically feasible.
  • Right to Object: You can object to the processing of your personal data on grounds relating to your particular situation, especially where processing is based on legitimate interests.

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, including submitting a request for access, rectification, or deletion of your personal data, please follow the procedures outlined in this privacy policy. We will need to verify your identity before processing such requests to ensure the security of your personal data.

7. Access, Modification, and Deletion of Personal Information

In our continuous commitment to empower you with control over your personal data, we have established a clear process that enables you to access, modify, or request the deletion of your information. This process includes:

  1. Accessing Your Data: You have the right to request a summary of the personal data we hold about you. Upon submitting a formal request, we will provide you with an accessible report that details this information, along with the purposes for which it is being processed.
  2. Modifying Your Data: If you believe any information we maintain is inaccurate or incomplete, you can request corrections or updates. We encourage you to review your details periodically and keep them up-to-date to ensure the integrity and effectiveness of our service.
  3. Deleting Your Data: Should you decide that you no longer wish to be associated with our services, you may request the deletion of your personal data. Once we receive such a request, and following a verification process to confirm your identity, we will take prompt action to remove your data from our active systems. Please note that some information may be retained in backup or archival systems for a limited period as required by law.

All requests for access, modification, or deletion should be made in writing and will be processed within the timeframes stipulated by applicable law. Our team is committed to ensuring that your requests are handled efficiently and securely.

8. Data Transfers and International Processing

In the course of our service delivery, your personal data may be transferred to and processed in jurisdictions outside of your own. We adhere strictly to the legal requirements governing cross-border data transfers, ensuring that when personal data is transferred internationally, it is protected by appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses or other approved mechanisms.

All international transfers are conducted in accordance with the prevailing data protection laws applicable, ensuring that your data is securely managed and you retain your privacy rights regardless of data location. We make every effort to minimize any potential risks associated with cross-border data flows.
